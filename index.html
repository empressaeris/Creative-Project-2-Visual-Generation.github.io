Creative Project 2 - Visual Generation

Artistic Statement

The idea of computers making our quality of life better is a concept that I always found untrue. As time goes on, I think it’s very much possible that computers; whether it be gaming, connecting to others on platforms like YouTube or Instagram, or even just how computers are used for hobbies such as art or making music. There’s going to be a point where technology will consume us, our friends, family and even our pets.

I wanted this concept of being fully wrapped and entangled in technology to be shown in my art. Whether it’s the idea that the physical forms of computer parts—wires, keyboards, computer screens, etc. becoming all-consuming. Whether to keep our technology running. Or, its physical form is actually inescapable.

However, the works themselves are meant to look somewhat photorealistic. Like, still images of a person, or their pets, and sometimes even just a lone animal, attached to their hardware by a multitude of wires. Almost coming from their bodies or at least, their bodies almost covered in wires to keep their technology going. Almost in a desperate attempt to keep using whatever piece of technology going. As they themselves look completely dystopian. While they are focused on either their task at hand or just existing with the wires and pieces of hardware surrounding them. Where each person or animal simply stays tied to their tech. In an almost inescapable way.

   

Artistic Influcenes

As for my inspiration, I took a concept from an anime called, “Ghost in the Shell.” Where the main character, Major Motoko Kusanagi, is a human living within a cybernetic body or “shell,” as it’s referred in-universe. However, there’s many depictions of Major Kusanagi where she has wires connecting her body—and as a result her mind—to people or other machines. Although the anime has this action of her connecting to other beings with wires looking very clean. They do sometimes wrap all around her. Or, occasionally there’s a string of multiple wires coming from the back of her neck. There’s also many instances where Kusanagi is resting. At times in a bed of some kind where she can connect to another person’s body. And there are also times where she’s getting modifications done. Either in a couch or sitting on the floor. Giving the idea that she’s in a relaxed environment, being connected either to a machine or another person’s mind allowed me to create poses for my subjects.

However, there are other works that I wanted to build upon. Since the images in question where going to be photographic in nature. Looking almost cinematic. I wanted to bring about how dystopian Pearl from the original Blade movie looked. But in a more realistic fashion. As Pearl’s character design was very overdone. However, Pearl is surrounded by technology: monitors all around the room and wires hanging in the background. There’s also one image that comes to mind when thinking about how people just “exist” with their technology and it surrounding them completely. It’s a photo of author Yoshihiro Togashi. Who is the created of Hunter x Hunter. He has a very famous picture of himself just surrounded by trash and tech. Just looking at a TV as though everything is normal.
